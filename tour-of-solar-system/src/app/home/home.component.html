Our solar system, a swirling cosmic dance, boasts a variety of celestial bodies, including the Sun, planets, moons, asteroids, and comets, all orbiting the Sun a central star.

The Sun, a giant ball of fire, is the heart of our solar system, and its gravity keeps everything in place. Eight planets follow nearly circular paths, Pluto, now a dwarf planet, orbits in a more elongated path, and is located in the Kuiper Belt, a region beyond Neptune teeming with icy bodies.
